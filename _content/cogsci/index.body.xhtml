My name is Ben Cipollini. I'm a 7th year (whaaat?) Ph.D candidate in the Cognitive Science Department at UC San Diego.

In my research, I use computational modeling as a way to fuse data from behavioral psychology and neuroscience and test hypotheses about how the left and right sides of the brain differ (they do) and interact (they do). I use those models to make predictions about the structure and function of the human brain.

I have a diverse set of interests outside of academia, and my experiences in and out of graduate school have led me to create talks on a number of different topics, including "what is human"?, about morality in science, among others.
