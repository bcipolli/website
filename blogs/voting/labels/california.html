Prop D: Alcohol Consumption on Beaches

This proposition seems entirely idealistic. It has nothing to do with budgets or anything. Interestingly, neither the Republican party NOR the Democratic party take an official position on this proposition!

So here we are. On the one hand, democracy demands choice. Leave it to people to do the right thing. Self-govern. On the other hand, democracy demands education and exercise of self-control, and neither of those things are happening.

If democracy was really about choice, we wouldn't have a drinking age, nor would we outlaw drugs or do a million of the other big-brother things that we do. So, I don't believe in the ideal. Arguing ideals is vacuous.

Then, what we argue is ... is alcoholic consumption bringing down the quality of our beaches? Would our beaches be better off without alcohol consumption? I'd say that, without a clear answer, we should allow things. Unless they're clearly problematic, we have no reason to impose laws and force ourselves to try and enforce them.

I haven't heard of any good argument on why our beaches would be better off without alcohol. I can see some small pluses (maybe safer, less rowdy crowd?), but also minuses (fewer people, less lively, less hot-spotty).

I don't see any good arguments at smartvoter.org either (just found the site; awesome!), so I vote NO.

Prop S: Funding for schools

The Democratic Party says YES on S; Republican Party say NO.

YES seems easy: $2 billion for education (although Wifi in every classroom seems completely unnecessary), no tax increase (just an extension of an existing tax program). See http://www.edforchildren.com/img/home/invite_oct15.pdf for a slightly more in depth view on YES

NO is harder to find arguments for. In fact, I just emailed comments@sandiegorepublicans.org:
Hi,

I'm trying to figure out my position on Prop S for the upcoming election and can't find any arguments for saying NO on Prop S. I see that the Republican Party's official endorsement on "S" is "NO". Is there somewhere I could see the reasoning behind that position so that I can make an educated decision myself?

Thank you,
Ben Cipollini
We'll see what that turns up.

------------------------------------------------------

OK, now that I've found SmartVoter.org, I've got everything I need. The "NO" argument is weak--even if they're right that $100 million is being diverted, that's only 5% of the need that they specify. Tiny! Their rebuttal is weak, their argument is weak, THEY ARE WEAK! AND, I'm not going to be a homeowner anytime soon, so this is great for ME!

YES ON PROP S!!

Mission Statement

I've got my absentee ballot in front of me. On 90% of the things I can vote on, I have no information other than the 3 sentence blurb on the ballot itself. How many people like myself vote based on these blurbs? Where do the blurbs come from? Is this really democracy?

I submit that it's not democracy unless each voter is educated. The whole "vote!" movement has it backwards--the first step is to get EDUCATED on what's happening. If after being educated you're not motivated to vote, then I will wish that I'd be living your life.

Each post in this blog will find bipartisan information on a single voting issue on the ballot. I'll be rockin' and rollin' through them, posting up links, thoughts, then a reasoned final decision, until I finish each and every entry in my California ballot.
