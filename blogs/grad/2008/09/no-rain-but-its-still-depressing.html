No Rain but it's Still Depressing

Hey all,

The Good
I'm in Seattle for a week. It's been 3 years since I've been here, and I have been looking forward to meeting up with old friends. Fortunately, there are two weeks off between the end of summer classes and the start of fall classes. Even more fortunately, I could afford to buy a plane ticket and rent a car for the week. And most fortunately, I had just the right number of friends to visit so that I could spend at least an evening with each person individually.

The Bad
Alas, the "unfortunates" are winning out: unfortunately, all my friends have real jobs and can't meet during the day. Unfortunately, I pulled the details of this trip together at the last second and didn't create a strong schedule to really make solid plans. And unfortunately, I don't have phone numbers and many of my friends aren't getting back to me via email!

The Ugly
With that said, I've been in classic "screw you!" mode, avoiding harping on what's NOT happening by staying focused on making other cool things happen. So far--I hiked up the backside (no, not ass; backside!) of Mt. St. Helens and did some hiking at Mt. Ranier. Also got 4 lbs of jerky at Stewart's Meat Market in Yelm (it's locally semi-famous! hot damn!). The less I give away to friends, the more I'll take back with me. Then again, this is the jerky that traumatized me... so maybe that's not for the best either. So far, I'm dealing.

The ... Actual Point
The REAL point of this post is to publish some photos from the trip. I did the work to patch together panoramic photos this morning. Here's a few to give a flavor of what I've been up to over the last few days.

The P.S.
Only partly inspired by Bill Pullman's "We will not go quietly into the night!" speech in Independence Day, I will continue to try and get in touch with my friends for dinner, lunch, or hiking. I will say what I want, I will show disappointment if I don't get it, and that's OK! :D
