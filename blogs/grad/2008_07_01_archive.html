The Groping Grad

I'm starting my second year in graduate school. Many say that grad school is about narrowing your focus, doing research, ... and realizing how little you know, how little you will know, and how hard it is to even maintain the knowledge that you do have. Ugh.

I'm in graduate school asking some questions about this life. Most of them end in "fuck":

  • "Why the fuck?" - The classic question for graduate students. We're a curious bunch; it can be used for exclamations about nature ("Why the fuck is every bit of the brain connected to every other bit??"). We're also a bunch of whiny bitches, and this gem can be used for classes and assignments ("Why the fuck do we have to read 120 pages of dry, technical journal papers for tomorrow's class?")
  • "How the fuck?" - Really popular in cognitive science, where we don't seem to know our asses from our heads. "How the fuck does the brain create consciousness?" "How the fuck can he talk when he's got that giant hole in his brain?" Also critical in getting any actual work done, as complaining is step #1 in the long process of doing any work whatsoever. "How the fuck can I do a paper for this class, generate results from my research, and prepare this talk for tomorrow??"
  • "Who the fuck?" - A severely underused question, "Who the fuck?" is useful for blaming ("Who the fuck forgot to clean the EEG cap?"), researching ("Who the fuck wrote that paper on the relationship between the BOLD response and LFPs?"), and, most critically, procrastinating and, critically, distracting others from your failure to get anything done ("Who the fuck was telling us about autism again? Remember that talk, that ruled.")
  • "When the fuck?" - An extremely dangerous question, because the answer is either going to be "yesterday, and you blew it" or "a lot sooner than you can handle, buddy." Best thing to do is to forget all dates as soon as possible. "When the fuck is the symposium submission deadline?" "When the fuck did you tell me to change the figure on this poster?"
  • "What the fuck?" - The mother of all "fuck" questions. Grad school is hard, confusing, bewildering. Ask questions of your advisor ("What the fuck do you want me to work on?"), your classmates ("What the fuck are we supposed to do?"), life itself ("What the fuck am I doing here?"), or to yourself ("WHAT THE FUCK?"). In a related note, this question can be reformulated into a exclamatory sentence, with a high reusability factor: for use when research doesn't work ("What THE FUCK!"), when you lock yourself out of your office while working late on Saturday night ("Oh, what the fuck!"), or with your hands and eyes raised to the sky when you just can't take it any more ("WHAT THE FUCK!!").
Bottom line--graduate school is about groping around in the dark for answers. Whether it be groping through endless virtual piles of electronic papers for data that might relate to your questions, groping through data you've collected for an actual result that you can report, or groping yourself because you just don't have the time, look, or recently cleansed body to attract a woman, be sure that you will be struggling, flailing, grasping, and groping every step of the way.

Fuck. If only.
