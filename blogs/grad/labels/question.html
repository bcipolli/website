Time's running short, and I'm TIRED!

... but "the question" continues to be a moving target. I "landed" on my current general topic area this morning, but have been shifting around and around in what exactly I can do and, just as importantly, how I will justify it.

Rather than always trying to justify things by mentioning the biggest picture items ("why do we have two hemispheres?"), I've finally nailed my question down to a specific motive: What would we learn if we tried to modify an existing model of the thalamo-cortical architecture to account for timing-related parameters of white-matter connectivity?

I guess it can pay to "hide" your exterior motive for a bit, and to simply talk about more immediate / proximate benefits of research. It's hard to take an idea that comes from a big question and to fit it under a much smaller question. In this case, I'm actually taking my interest (timing issues in inter-hemispheric communication), finding an answerable question about it (how would the corpus callosum be used in a specific model of the brain), and then incoroporating that question into a research goal that is useful to a wider audience.

Or at least, we'll see. I've read some, thought some, and written very little.
